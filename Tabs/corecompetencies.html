List of Core Compentencies

I have a master degree in Nanoscience, where I specialized in the field of materials science. Nanoscience in short is the focus on matter on the nanoscale. In other words atoms. Therefore there is a natural focus on techniques used for characterizing stuff on this level. Nanoscience is still in its infancy education wise, hence it is, from my experience, not that known in general. However it allows for a greater understanding of the macroscropic properties observed. One of the strengths of a Nanoscience is the true interdisciplinary nature. By that I mean interdisciplinarity is used a lot these days, hence the meaning has been a bit diluted, but a Nanoscientist has knowledge from all the classical areas of natural sciences (physics, chemistry, biology and molecular biology) and has a strong foundation to develop these competencies with a unique perspective and inspiration from other fields.

If you want to know more, I do encourage you to read more about Nanoscience, but in short you cannot really go wrong, if you choose a Nanoscientist.

Chemistry

Chemistry was my main work area both during my bachelors and masters project. I focused on using a broad range of synthesis strategies and rather than sticking to one single compound I worked with several. However I aimed at synthesizing perovskites due to their interesting properties and also double perovskites. Furthermore I also had a big focus on osmates. The different synthesis methods I worked with were:

  • Solid state synthesis - oxidating, reducing, inert environment
  • Quartz tube synthesis and crystal growth - great control over environment and possibility for a small pressure
  • Sol gel chemistry - Pechini method, aqueous solutions with different metal coordinators
  • Wet chemistry
  • High pressure synthesis - extreme conditions that resembles those found inside the Earth
Furthermore I characterized all my powder samples with PXRD, hence I have a huge experience with powder characterization. Lastly apart from my obligations to synthesize these compound, I also worked with a bunch of practical projects due to my experience with CAD and machining.

Since chemistry is an area I have worked a lot, I am more focused on applying the methods I learned where I can to my other interest. However I do want to improve my abilities in organic chemistry, since it ties neatly together with coatings for corrosion protection purposes. Also it is a topic that I have not worked with in a while, hence I want to get my competencies within this field refreshed.

Corrosion theory and lifetime prediction:

I got interested in corrosion due to my job at Terma. The goal with predict lifetime of products under galvanic corrosion and screen materials in order to improve both product quality and determine when a potential maintenance was required. Since Terma has antennas placed in different environments all around the world, it was critically important that the model was generalized and could be applied to anywhere. Hence unlike HACT experiments and the like, it was necessary to determine corrosion spreading throughout the entire process, rather than pictures of the end result or in timed intervals. After Terma most of the theory has pretty much stayed the same and instead I have been working on designing test setups by learning how to design electronic circuits and programming microcontrollers.
Most of my work within this field is still to come but I am actively gathering data from eight experiments at the same time. However I will note that I do not have enough time to do all of this due to my job search and other obligations, since I am doing it in my spare time and I have a limited amount of money available.
Therefore If you are interested in corrosion and possible want to establish a partnership, feel free to contant me.

Since I am actively gathering data, I am working more on software development and also upgrading my setups to be both larger, have the ability to test more paramemters and control the surrouding environment better. In short:

  • Corrosion test design and development for real-time monitoring
