Materials in Chemistry

Materials chemistry is the field I have worked the most in as stated above. My main focus in my research was High Pressure Synthesis and in general solid state synthesis. The basis for this is simply to mix powders and let them react by heating. However things get more complicated in practice, since usually there are requirements to the surrondings and there exists a broad range of methods for controlling these. In short you end up with a powder or crystal which can be characterized with different techniques. The most common is definitely PXRD, since it allows for characterization of ones powder. There are also wet chemistry and sol gel chemistry which ultimately lead to a powder product, but a through a very different reaction pathway with its own advantages and disadvantages. All of this can be found in the tab "Chemistry". Other than my experimental work I have also had a broad range of courses in chemistry, which helps me understand the properties that can be observed. The most important courses I have had in chemistry are:

  • Inorganic Chemistry.
  • Experimental Materials Chemistry.
  • Organic Chemistry.
  • Soft Condensed Matter.
  • Polymer Chemistry.
  • Crystallopgraphy.
The theories used in chemistries are obviously not the same, since their focuses are very different. However in many cases there is a overlap, since for example Heat Capacity requires an understanding of physics to decipher the results obtained in experiment.

Corrosion - Application of theory in practice

Parallel to my studies I worked once a week at Terma, where I was responsible for developing predicting lifetimes for objects under galvanic corrosion and screening materials for their corrosion resistance. This was attempted by developing a mathematical model, which described the material and external parameters in generalized terms in order to be able to be independent of the location, since Terma has equipment in many parts of the world. Furthermore one more advantage is that the corrosion experiments would not take long, where standard experiments such as Highly Accelerated Corrosion Tests (HACT) are quite time consuming. The ambition was to be able to screen a material within half day. However one major drawback was that much of the work had to be done from the bottom up, hence due to my limited time I was not able to finish the research. The research I did was based on my experience and theoretical knowledge from chemistry and physics. Especially my knowledge of electrodynamic and mass transport and reaction knowledge from chemistry helped me in this endeavour.

Following Terma I have been working on improving the work I started. Mostly in the experimental setup since I did not have any equipment available to me. Therefore I had to develop a small compact setup, which could do what I needed with a high precision. The first Corrosion Setup I made can be seen here. I am currently using this along with one similar to conduct eight experiments concurrently. Nevertheless due to the limitations with this I have been working on improving this, which also can be found in Practical.

Since my main barrier to overcome has been within my experimental setup after Terma much of the theoretical work has been on standby. However with the experimental setups getting "close" to being done, then I will soon turn my attention towards theoretical and software development in order to simulate corrosion spreading based on the parameters I used at Terma.
