Data Analysis and Simulation

Matlab

Matlab was the first language I ever learned how to program in. Before then I only experimented a bit with macros in Excel. This happened during my second year at university in the course "Computational Physics". In this course I learned about a lot of the basics in programming and how to do plot stuff, solve equation for an unknown, integrate, solve differentiel equation and simulate. However it was not until I had to work with it at Terma that I really learned how things worked. For me this was the beginning of a much larger interest for programming in general and during the last couple of months at Terma, I learned how to work build and work with interfaces.

After Terma I continued my programming adventure and since I wanted to learn more about creating interfaces, I started making another one for my former supervisor at inorganic chemistry. This was meant to analyze data from high pressure experiments with different models for Equation of State (EoS) and make it easy to compare the results of these models to determine which one was best suited to describe the data. Other than that I helped some of the younger students with scripting and I wrote some myself. Most notably a plotting tool that automated the tedious process of plotting the results and bragg peaks/positions from rietveld refinements. This method is used all the time in inorganic chemistry for powder analysis, hence it would save a lot of time.

To summarize my most important competencies in Matlab are:

  • Analyzing data and plotting the results.
  • Solving (differentials) equations and integrating.
  • Making simulations.
  • Designing interfaces.

The design of interfaces made me more aware of Object-Oriented programming (OOP) and the concepts behind it. However it was not until I started learning Python that I really actually made "true" OOP. Ever since then I have been programming in Python, which will be covered below.

Microcontrollers and Test Design

A derivate of C++

Microcontrollers caught my interest due to making my own setup for corrosion experiments over long spans of time. You can see the Practical Work part of that endeavour. This section is devoted to shortly explain what I worked with on the programming side. Arduino/Teensy programming is based on a derivative of C++. Therefore I had to learn the basic concepts of C++. Luckily it is similar to what I had worked with in Python at that time, since it was before my FullStack developer course. In my case I worked with precise measurement and logging of corrosion voltage. Hence I worked with circular arrays, logging onto an SD card and ensuring it was connected before any measurement could start and of course defining all the logic for the inputs/outputs. Furthermore I experimented with IoT and setting up local servers in order to control the experiment based on the real-time monitoring and extract information during it. Lastly I implemented a start/pause button based on a simple counter. This is of course only a small fraction of the entire project, since the hard part is optimizing the outcome between the versatility in the programming part and understanding what electronics part to use and their physical limitations. In any case I will be more than happy to give further details in case test design is of interest to you.
